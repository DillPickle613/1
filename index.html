Stop Chinese Organ Trafficking

Definition of Organ Trafficking

Organ trafficking is defined as illegal harvest, transport, and/or use of human organs. In China, it is a billion dollar industry. Although it generates a profit for the Chinese government, it is extremely cruel and harmful. Each year, Chinese hospitals illegally perform over 60,000 organ transplants. Most of the organs used are taken from those not aligned with the Chinese government’s political or religious ideals. People such as those who follow Falun Gong are targeted based on their beliefs, and executed by the government in order to replenish the supply of organs for transplant.

The Chinese organ trafficking goes against international law declared by the United Nations. According to the UN, it is a direct violation of human rights.

Persecution of Falun Gong

The main source of these illegally transplanted organs is Falun Gong practitioners. Falun Gong is a disciplinary centre teaching the Buddhist religion. China is prosecuting them because they think that Falun Gong practitioners have too much influence on Chinese citizens. Additionally, the Chinese government fears that Falun Gong practitioners can not be controlled by their propaganda. In the Chinese government’s perspective, this necessitates the killing and harvesting of organs of Falun Gong practitioners.

This map of China shows prevelance of persecution.

Evidence of Persecution

The Chinese government has created false claims stating that they don't use this practice anymore and have been accused of illegal organ trafficking by the Vatican. Various people state that the Chinese are still using this practice everyday. There has also been a book written in 2009 called Bloody Harvest, which goes into depth about these terrible killings. A phone conversation recorded states this:

Caller: I want to know how long [the patients] have to wait [for a liver transplant].

Dr. Dai: The supply of organs we have, we have every day. We do them every day.

Caller: We want fresh, live ones.

Dr. Dai: They are all live, all live…

Caller: I heard some come from those who practice Falun Gong, those who are very healthy.

Dr. Dai: Yes, we have. I can’t talk openly to you over the phone.

Caller: If you can find me this type, I am coming very soon.

Dr. Dai: It’s OK. Please come.

This conversation is between a Chinese doctor and an investigative reporter. The doctor admits to the Chinese government’s illegal organ transplants and confirms that the organs come from Falun Gong practitioners.
